# BSC AI Factory — External Resources

This repository provides a curated collection of **external** software tools, libraries, guides, and reference materials relevant to the activities of the **BSC AI Factory**.

These resources include:

- External documentation, best-practice guides, and learning materials  
- Open-source tools and frameworks widely used in AI, HPC, MLOps, and data engineering  
- Public examples, templates, and reference implementations  
- Links to community-maintained projects considered useful for development, research, and experimentation  

No internal or proprietary BSC material is included in this repository.  
All entries consist exclusively of **external, publicly accessible resources** selected and curated by the AI Factory team.

Below you will find the main categories used to organise these resources.

---

# 📁 Categories of External Tools

Each category below includes a short explanation of its purpose and scope.

---

## 1. **Machine Learning & LLM Frameworks**

See: [ML-FRAMEWORKS.md](ML-FRAMEWORKS.md)

Core frameworks and libraries used to build, train, or run machine learning models, from traditional ML to large-scale foundation models.  
These frameworks form the basis of model development across AI workflows.

---

## 2. **Workflow & Orchestration**

See: [WORKFLOW-ORCHESTRATION.md](WORKFLOW-ORCHESTRATION.md)

Tools designed to manage pipelines, automate execution, schedule complex tasks, and ensure reproducible workflows.  
They help structure multi-step computational processes in a maintainable, traceable way.

---

## 3. **MLOps Platforms & Experiment Management**

See: [MLOPS-PLATFORMS.md](MLOPS-PLATFORMS.md)

Systems that support experiment tracking, model versioning, artifact management, and deployment lifecycles.  
They enable reproducibility, governance, and structured operational practices for ML projects.

---

## 4. **Distributed Training & HPC Integration**

See: [DISTRIBUTED-HPC.md](DISTRIBUTED-HPC.md)

Libraries that enable scaling model training across GPUs, nodes, or full HPC systems.  
They provide parallelisation strategies essential for high-performance AI workloads.

---

## 5. **Data Engineering & Storage Systems**

See: [DATA-ENGINEERING.md](DATA-ENGINEERING.md)

Frameworks and tools for processing, transforming, storing, and querying large-scale datasets.  
These systems supply reliable, high-quality data pipelines for AI and HPC environments.

---

## 6. **Federated Learning Frameworks**

See: [FEDERATED-LEARNING.md](FEDERATED-LEARNING.md)

Tools that support decentralised or privacy-preserving machine learning without centralising data.  
These frameworks are used when collaboration is required across distributed or sensitive datasets.

---

## 7. **Benchmarks & Evaluation**

See: [BENCHMARKS-EVALUATION.md](BENCHMARKS-EVALUATION.md)

Frameworks used to assess model performance, measure quality, and compare results against standardised benchmarks.  
They ensure consistency and transparency in evaluation methodologies.

---

## 8. **Visualization & Observability**

See: [VISUALIZATION-OBSERVABILITY.md](VISUALIZATION-OBSERVABILITY.md)

Tools for monitoring, visualising, and analysing metrics related to model performance, system behaviour, and operational health.  
They support debugging, optimisation, and production oversight.

---

## 9. **DevOps, Deployment & Infrastructure**

See: [DEVOPS-INFRASTRUCTURE.md](DEVOPS-INFRASTRUCTURE.md)

General-purpose infrastructure technologies supporting automation, deployment, and environment management.  
These tools form the foundation for scalable, maintainable AI and HPC operations.

---
